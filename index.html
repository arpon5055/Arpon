My Journey at Biddyaniketon School (2012–2024)

From the very first day I stepped into the gates of Biddyaniketon School in 2012, to the emotional farewell in 2024 — this place was more than just a school to me. It was my second home. Over the span of 12 long years, I didn’t just grow up academically, I grew up as a person. Every classroom, corridor, and corner of the school holds memories I deeply cherish. I was a proud member of the SSC Batch of 2024 — a batch full of energy, dreams, and, of course, mischief. I was known among my friends and teachers not just for leading from the front, but also for being the mastermind behind many fun moments and playful chaos. Whether it was organizing group activities, pulling off light-hearted pranks, or simply bringing smiles to people’s faces — those memories make me smile to this day. Biddyaniketon wasn’t just a part of my childhood — it shaped me. And no matter where life takes me, a big part of me will always belong to those unforgettable school days.

Journey as a volunteer of
Red Crescent Unit,Narayanganj.

I have been an active volunteer of the Red Crescent Youth, Narayanganj unit, for a long time. Being part of this humanitarian organization has been a life-changing experience for me. Over the years, I have received training in several essential areas, including First Aid, Anticipatory Action (AA), Climate Change Adaptation & Nature-Based Solutions (CCA & NBS), Stay Safe, and Psychological First Aid (PFA). These trainings have equipped me with practical skills to respond in emergencies and support communities during both crises and peace times. Through Red Crescent, I’ve had the opportunity to serve people, grow as a responsible youth, and contribute meaningfully to society.

What is life?!

Life is nothing but a fleeting journey between birth and death — a path we all must walk, uncertain of its length but rich in moments. From our first breath to our last, we experience joy, pain, love, loss, growth, and decay. It is not the destination that defines us, but the choices we make along the way, the connections we form, and the meaning we find in even the smallest of experiences. Life teaches us that everything is temporary — success, sorrow, youth, time — and in this impermanence lies its beauty. To truly live is to embrace the present, learn from the past, and walk forward without fear of the inevitable end, knowing that the journey itself is what matters most.
